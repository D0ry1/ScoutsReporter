Scouts Reporter — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 14 June 2026

Scouts Reporter ("the app") is a Windows desktop tool that helps Scout volunteers generate compliance reports (criminal record checks/DBS, learning, and permits) for members in the part of the Scouts hierarchy they are already authorised to view.

The short version

The app runs entirely on your own computer. It does not send your data, or any members' data, to the app's developer or to any third party. The developer operates no servers and receives no personal data of any kind.

What the app accesses

When you sign in with your own Scouts membership account, the app connects to the official Scouts membership system (membership.scouts.org.uk) and reads only the membership information you are already permitted to see — for example members' names, membership numbers, roles, criminal record check/learning/permit status, and contact email addresses. It accesses this through the same official Scouts services the membership website uses.

What is stored, and where

  • All data is processed and stored locally on your device.
  • Your Scouts sign-in token is stored locally and encrypted using Windows data protection (DPAPI), so you don't have to sign in every time. You can remove it at any time by signing out or uninstalling the app.
  • Report data is cached locally to speed up the app. Any reports or exports you create are saved only where you choose to save them, on your device.
  • Settings are stored under your local application data folder.

What is shared

Nothing is shared with the developer or third parties. Network connections are made only to:

  • the official Scouts membership services, to sign you in and read the data you request; and
  • for the directly-downloaded version, GitHub, solely to check for and download app updates — no personal data is sent in this check.

The app contains no analytics, tracking, or telemetry.

Your responsibilities and the data controller

The Scout Association is the data controller for Scouts membership data. Scouts Reporter is a tool you use to access data you are authorised to access. You are responsible for handling any reports or exported data in line with The Scout Association's data protection policy and applicable data protection law (including the UK GDPR) — for example, storing them securely and only using them for legitimate volunteering purposes.

Deleting your data

Uninstalling the app removes its locally stored token, cache and settings. You may also delete the stored sign-in token at any time without uninstalling.

Children

The app is intended for use by adult Scout volunteers carrying out their volunteering role. It is not directed at children.

Changes

This policy may be updated from time to time; the "last updated" date above will change accordingly.

Contact
